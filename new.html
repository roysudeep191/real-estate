Real estate is a type of property that includes land and any permanent structures or improvements attached to it, such as buildings, homes, or natural resources. It can be used for residential, commercial, or industrial purposes. Real estate is different from personal property, which is not permanently attached to the land, such as vehicles, furniture, or jewelry. Real estate is often a valuable asset that can appreciate over time. The value of real estate is a leading indicator of an economy's health, and the real estate market impacts millions of jobs. Here are some things to know about real estate: Real estate professionals Real estate agents and realtors help individuals and businesses navigate the real estate transaction process. Real estate businesses Real estate businesses maintain databases of properties for sale or lease, and may also manage properties for use. Real estate valuation The value of a property is not necessarily equal to its cost or price. For example, a house's sales price might be $150,000, but its value could be higher or lower depending on factors like the condition of the house. Home equity loans Taking out a loan against your home's equity can be risky. If real estate prices decline, you could end up owing more on the loan than the house is worth.
